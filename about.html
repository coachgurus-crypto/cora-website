Our story

We built Cora because PCOS care felt cold. It shouldn't.

One in ten people with ovaries has PCOS — and most are handed a confusing diagnosis and left to figure it out alone. We're here to change that, gently.

Cora started with a simple, frustrating truth: the tools meant to help us understand our bodies often make us feel worse. Period trackers assume a tidy 28-day cycle. Wellness apps push streaks and shame. And almost all of them quietly sell the most intimate data you'll ever generate.

For the millions of people living with PCOS — with cycles that don't follow the rules, symptoms that come and go, and treatments that take months to show — that approach simply doesn't work.

"We wanted an app that felt like a calm friend who happens to be great with patterns — not a coach yelling about streaks."

So we built Cora: a tracker that meets your body where it actually is, surfaces what's genuinely helping, and treats your data like the private medical record it is. No ads. No data brokers. No judgment.

We're a small, independent team backed by clinicians and people who live with PCOS every day. We're not trying to replace your doctor — we're trying to help you walk into that appointment with clarity instead of a shoebox of confusing notes.

What we believe

The principles behind every screen.

Kindness over streaks

We will never use guilt, streaks or fear to drive engagement. A two-tap day is a good day. Missing a week is fine. Your worth isn't a logging percentage.

Privacy is non-negotiable

Your health data is encrypted and never sold or shared with advertisers. It's yours — to export or delete, completely, whenever you choose.

Honesty, even when it's quiet

We show real progress windows and clear prices. No miracle claims, no fake urgency, no dark patterns to trap you in a subscription.

Built with, not just for

Every feature is shaped by people who live with PCOS and the clinicians who treat it. If it doesn't help a real person, it doesn't ship.
